Digital Transformation Programme — Monthly Steering Update
Month 14 of 24 | June 2025 | Prepared for: Executive Steering Committee

Programme Overview
------------------
The Digital Transformation Programme (DTP) is a two-year initiative to modernise core business
operations across Finance, HR, and Customer Services. The programme is structured across five
workstreams, each with an assigned senior responsible owner (SRO).

RAG Status Summary
------------------
Workstream 1 — Finance Systems Modernisation     GREEN
Workstream 2 — Customer Portal Rebuild           AMBER
Workstream 3 — HR Self-Service Platform          GREEN
Workstream 4 — Data & Reporting Infrastructure  AMBER
Workstream 5 — Cyber Security Uplift             RED

Overall Programme Status: AMBER

Key Achievements This Month
-----------------------------
- Workstream 1: Automated month-end reconciliation went live on 2 June. Early results show
  a reduction in manual reconciliation effort from 3.5 days to 4 hours. Finance team sign-off
  received.

- Workstream 3: HR self-service portal passed user acceptance testing with a satisfaction
  score of 82% (threshold: 75%). Rollout to remaining 1,200 staff scheduled for July.

- Workstream 5 (partial): Privileged access management (PAM) tooling deployed to 60% of
  endpoints ahead of schedule. Remaining 40% blocked pending device refresh — see risks below.

Issues and Escalations
-----------------------
Issue DTP-041 (Workstream 2 — Customer Portal):
The third-party CRM vendor has notified the programme that their API v2 will be deprecated in
September 2025, two months earlier than the contracted support end date. The portal rebuild is
currently integrating against API v2. The team has raised a formal dispute with the vendor;
legal review is underway. Worst-case scenario requires a 6-week development rework.
Impact: potential delay to Workstream 2 go-live from October to December 2025.
Escalation required: Steering Committee decision on contingency budget (estimated £85k).

Issue DTP-044 (Workstream 4 — Data & Reporting):
Data migration from the legacy data warehouse has uncovered 340,000 records with data quality
issues (duplicate customer IDs, missing postcode fields, inconsistent date formats). The data
cleansing effort was not accounted for in the original project scope. An additional 3 weeks of
engineering time has been absorbed, pushing the reporting dashboard go-live from week 28 to
week 32.

Issue DTP-047 (Workstream 5 — Cyber Security):
The planned device refresh for 800 endpoints required to complete PAM rollout has been delayed
by procurement. New hardware delivery now expected week 30, against a programme need of week 24.
This delay puts the Cyber workstream critical path at risk and is the primary reason for the RED
status. SRO has escalated to the COO.
